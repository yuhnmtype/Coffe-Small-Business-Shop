How to Brew the Perfect
Cà Phê Sữa Đá at Home

By Huy Phan 12 March 2025 5 min read

The Phin filter is one of the most elegant brewing devices ever invented. It does exactly two things: holds coffee grounds, and lets gravity take over. And yet, almost everyone who brews with one for the first time makes at least one of three mistakes.

The Three Common Mistakes

1. Using the wrong grind. Phin brewing needs a medium-coarse grind — not espresso fine, not French press coarse. If your coffee takes less than 3 minutes to drip, it's too coarse. More than 6 minutes, too fine.

2. Under-filling the condensed milk. Cà phê sữa đá is a bold drink. A typical Vietnamese recipe calls for 2–3 tablespoons of sweetened condensed milk per cup. Most first-timers add one. Don't be shy.

3. Not preheating the Phin. Pour a tiny splash of hot water through the filter before adding your grounds. This removes any metallic taste and warms the metal so your brew temperature stays stable.

"Good cà phê sữa đá is about patience. The drip should be slow, almost meditative. If you're in a hurry, make something else."
— Minh Nguyễn, Head Roaster

The Recipe

You'll need:

  • 1 Phin filter (any size, 8–10g capacity ideal)
  • 20g medium-dark Vietnamese robusta or robusta-arabica blend
  • 150ml hot water at 92–96°C
  • 2–3 tbsp sweetened condensed milk
  • A tall glass with ice

Steps: Add condensed milk to the glass. Set Phin on top. Add grounds and press the filter gently. Pour 30ml of hot water first to bloom for 30 seconds. Then add the remaining water and cover with the Phin lid. Let it drip — about 4–5 minutes total. Pour over ice, stir, drink immediately.

That's it. No fancy equipment, no scales required (though a scale helps). Just a good Vietnamese robusta blend and enough time to let physics do its thing.
